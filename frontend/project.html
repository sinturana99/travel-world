Welcome to Westbengal

Kalimpong

Kalimpong became an important centre for trade with Tibet due to the closeness of the town to the Jelepla Pass which allowed access to Central Tibet
Santiniketan

Santiniketan, popularly known today as a university town, a hundred miles to the north of Kolkata, was originally an ashram built by Debendranath Tagore, where anyone, irrespective of caste and creed, could come and spend time meditating on the one Supreme God.
Darjeeling

Darjeeling is situated on a long, narrow mountain ridge of the Sikkim Himalayas that descends abruptly to the bed of the Great Rangit River.
Cooch Behar

Before 28th August 1949, Cooch Behar was a Princely State ruled by the king of Cooch Behar, who had been a feudatory ruler under British Government.
Malda

The Malda Museum has a great collection of the region's architectural and anthropological specimens. During the 18th century, Malda was the seat of prosperous cotton and silk industries.
Chandannagar

Established as a French settlement in 1673, the town of Chandannagar is situated by the River Hooghly and has turned into a popular tourist attraction today.

Murshidabad

Before the advent of British, the city of Murshidabad was the capital of Bengal.

Kolkata

Kolkata was the capital of the British India until 1911, when the capital was relocated to Delhi. Kolkata grew rapidly in the 19th century to become the second most important city of the British Empire after London and declared financial (commercial) capital of the British India.
